Key Definitions
Campaign Conv %
% of campaigns that received at least 1 approved review. G2 Sends = G2 emails customers on vendor's behalf. Vendor Sends = vendor creates a campaign LP on G2 — our visibility ends at LP creation. We track reviews that come through it but cannot confirm whether or how the vendor distributed the link.
Early Launchers
Profiles that created a campaign LP within 30 days of Starter purchase. Highest-intent cohort — they tried immediately.
Activated (5+ reviews)
Profile received 5+ approved reviews within 30 days of purchase. Two cuts reported: Campaign LP only and All Sources (any approved review on the product).
30-Day Time-Boxed
All review metrics are strictly bounded to 30 days from each customer's own Starter purchase date. Per-customer window — not a period-level aggregate.
Campaign LP Only
Reviews that came through a G2 review campaign landing page within 30 days. Strictly G2-campaign-attributable. The primary metric used throughout.
All Sources
Every approved review on the product within 30 days of Starter purchase — organic G2 discovery, campaign LP, in-app, or any other source. Used in Section 4 only.
P75 Reviews
75th percentile of reviews per campaign in a period. When P75 = 0, at least 75% of all campaigns produced zero reviews. Measures whether even average campaigns are working — not just the top performers.

The Causal Chain
Both review campaign channels are declining — each in a different way. Together they produce the same downstream effect: fewer reviews, lower activation.
Channel 1
G2 Sends declined steadily
Email suppression hit delivery reach. Conversion dropped from 30.6% in P1 to 25.2% in P4. G2 Sends as a share of all campaigns fell from 42% to 16%.
→
Channel 2
Vendor Sends volume up, quality collapsed
Vendor Sends grew 10× in campaigns — but conversion fell from 46% in P2 to 15.4% in P4. We track reviews through the LP but cannot confirm whether or how vendors distributed the link.
→
Effect
Review activation dropped
Customers launching in first 30 days activated at 24.3% in P2. Now 8.7%. More campaigns, fewer reviews, fewer activations. Customers are trying harder; both channels are failing them.

Vendor Sends was the stronger channel in P2 — 46% conversion, 6.85 avg reviews per campaign. By P4 it had grown to 1,107 campaigns in a single period while conversion fell to 15.4%. P75 reviews hit 0 — three quarters of all Vendor Sends campaigns produced nothing.

8
Summary & What We Know
The data tells a connected story across 16 months and 1,163 Starter profiles.
①
G2 Sends declined steadily
Email suppression reduced delivery reach. Conversion dropped from 30.6% (P1) to 25.2% (P4). G2 Sends as share of all campaigns fell from 42% to 16% as Vendor Sends grew.
②
Vendor Sends collapsed on quality
Grew from 101 campaigns (P1) to 1,107 (P4). But conversion fell from 46% in P2 to 15.4% in P4. P75 hit 0. We can only confirm LP creation — not distribution or why reviews stopped coming back.
③
Activation dropped as a direct result
Campaigns are the primary mechanism for getting reviews in the first 30 days. They stopped converting. 30-day campaign LP activation dropped from 24.3% (P2) to 8.7% (P4) for early launchers.
④
All-sources picture is better — but doesn't fix the campaign problem
When counting all approved reviews in 30 days, activation in P4 is 18.7% — 3× higher than campaign-only. Customers are finding other review paths. The campaign system is not the whole story, but it remains the lever G2 controls.
What we don't have: We cannot separate G2 rep-created campaign LPs (via myG2 observer mode) from vendor-created ones — the review_campaigns table has no creator field. We also cannot confirm vendor distribution behaviour for Vendor Sends. Both are known data gaps. Impact on Starter is believed to be minimal given its self-serve nature.
